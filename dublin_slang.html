10 DUBLIN SLANG WORDS AND PHRASES EXPLAINED IN ENGLISH

#1. D’yaknowhwatimeanlike

Meaning “Do you know what I mean like”? This is used constantly as a kind of punctuation between sentences.

"I haven't a clue what I'm talking about – D’yaknowhatimeanlike?"

#2. C'mere till I tell ya

Meaning “Come here until I tell you.”

This is usually said to indicate that something important about to be revealed (such as gossip!) "C'mere till I tell ya, yer man was out with yer wun last night."

#3. I’m lee fucking hungry

Meaning “I am awefully hungry”

#4. Ye bleeeeedin doooopeee ye

Meaning “You bleeding dope you” or “you silly billy,” or fool.

Yes … you’ve got it, “dope” does not have the same slang meaning as in the US, where it means something very cool or trendy. You can imagine our amusement at the tshirts and hats people wear emblazoned with the word “Dope.” "Ye missed the bus! You’re a bleeeedin’ doooope ye."

#5. Deadly

Contrary to its actual meaning, in Dublin, ‘deadly’ is the highest honour you can bestow upon a person.

Want to express admiration or delight in something? Then describe it as deadly. Want to give a new restaurant the thumbs up? Describe it as deadly. Want to make sure your new fella is into you? Make sure he thinks you’re deadly.

#6. Ya bleedin’ spanner

Meaning you are really quite useless, an idiot.

Some who really puts a spanner [wrench] in the works…get it! “Wha’ did you do that for ye bleeding spanner!”

#7. It is fookin banjaxed

Meaning “it is well beyond repair”

#8. You’re nothing but a bowsie

Meaning that your character really is most questionable

#9. Go ask me bollix

What it usually means: “Sir, please address your question to my testicles.”

Meaning in Dublin: All-purpose response to any question you don’t want to answer. “I’m asking you to have that on my desk by this afternoon.” “Ask me bollix.”
